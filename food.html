Local Food

Gudeg Solo

Gudeg Solo is a traditional Javanese dish originating from the city of Solo, Central Java. It is a savory and slightly sweet stew made primarily from young jackfruit, slow-cooked in coconut milk and a blend of aromatic spices such as coriander, garlic, and palm sugar. Unlike the Yogyakarta version, Gudeg Solo tends to be less sweet and includes more coconut milk, giving it a creamier and richer texture. Often served with rice, Gudeg Solo is accompanied by various side dishes such as opor ayam (chicken in coconut milk), tempeh, tofu, and hard-boiled eggs, making it a hearty and satisfying meal that reflects the rich culinary heritage of Central Java.
